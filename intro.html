About Me

I was born in Huanggang, Hubei, a small town in central China. The greatest river of China, Yangtze River, run directly across my frontyard, where I can fish all day in my childhood. I attended undergraduate in University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) and obtained my Bachelor of Science in chemical physics. After that, I moved to Johns Hopkins University (JHU) for my PhD in experimental electrocatalysis. Things aren't going well there, as quoted from my mentors, "try to synthesis a wire but got a tube, try to synthesis a tube but got a whatever chunk it is"

I then decided to quit my PhD in experimental chemistry and pursued a PhD in theoretical chemistry under Prof. David Yarkony in Johns Hopkins. At there I finally find myself a little bit more useful than I was. After obtaining my PhD in 2022, I am now a postdoctoral associate in University of Chicago in Prof. David Mazziotti's group.

My Reseach Interests and publications

I have a general interest in quantum physics & molecular physics/chemistry. Some of the topics I have worked on including quantum computing algorithm, quantum many-body theory, reduced density matrices, nonadiabatic molecular dynamics and neural network potential energy surfaces.
